Dipu Number Two

1996, Morshedul Islam

2hr 34min

Adventure

Dipu Number Two (Bengali: দীপু নাম্বার টু) is a 1996 Bangladeshi film directed by Morshedul Islam.[3] The screenplay was based on the 1984 novel of the same name by Muhammed Zafar Iqbal.

PLOT SUMMARY

Dipu (Arun Saha), a boy of about twelve years lives with his father (Bulbul Ahmed). Dipu's father is a government officer and because of his transferable job, they arrive at a picturesque town. Dipu immediately develops a liking to this new town and his new school. He makes a lot of friends but starts a feud with the school bully Tarique (Shubhashish) on the very first day. Tarique, unable to intimidate Dipu tries many tricks on him and even beats him up one day.
